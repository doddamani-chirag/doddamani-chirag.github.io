Holi

~ The Festival Of Colors

ABOUT HOLI HISTORY IMAGES

Holi, the festival of colours, has a lot of meaning for individuals. It is the triumph of good over evil, and it is a day to forget about our problems and make peace with everyone. People of all castes, creeds, and races get together on this day to enjoy the holiday with their loved ones and neighbours without prejudice. The event is held on the Purnima (full moon day) of Phalgun, a Hindu calendar month. This celebration is significant for people since it marks the end of winter and the beginning of spring.

Holika Dahan

        

Holika, sometimes also known as Simhika, was a demoness in scriptures. She was the sister of King Hiranyakashipu and the aunt of Prahlad.The story of Holika Dahan (Holika's death) signifies the triumph of righteousness over non-righteousness. Holika is associated with the annual bonfire on the night before Holi, the festival of colors.
